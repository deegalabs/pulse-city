# Market Landscape
> Phase: discover | Brand: pulse-city-next | Generated: 2026-04-15

---

Four overlapping markets define pulse.city's addressable air. None owns the specific intersection pulse.city is walking into.

## 1. Live-code music environments

**Definition.** Browser-first or desktop pattern languages where the code *is* the instrument and the performance is the act of typing.

**Key players / signals (2026).**
- **Strudel** — JS port of TidalCycles, browser-native, the technical parent pulse.city inherits from.
- **TidalCycles** — Haskell original, Alex McLean's ongoing research agenda, still the reference for "algorave" culture.
- **Sonic Pi** — Sam Aaron's Ruby-based environment, strongest education/accessibility signal ("blind and partially sighted people" is a homepage line).
- Algorave nights continue to run in Berlin, Tokyo, and São Paulo; the scene is small but self-renewing.

**Lia's world intersects here** at the toolmaker edge: she's seen Strudel on Are.na mood boards and in Lainchan-adjacent music threads. She respects the lineage but has never performed at an algorave. The lineage is *legitimizing*, not *inviting*.

**Trajectory.** Stable-but-small. Strudel's GitHub star graph is flat-linear, not hockey-stick; TidalCycles has been shipping for ~15 years; Sonic Pi is institutional now (Raspberry Pi bundled). The scene has cultural capital and zero commercial pressure — a safe lineage to borrow from, a weak lineage to depend on for reach.

## 2. Internet radio renaissance

**Definition.** Curated, human-flavored streaming radio that rejects algorithmic playlist culture. Low-infra, high-personality.

**Key players / signals (2026).**
- **SomaFM** — 40+ hand-curated stations, listener-supported ("April fundraising drive happening now" is front-of-site copy), Atlas Grotesk-adjacent functional typography, channel art as the only warm flourish.
- **Radio Garden** — Cesium WebGL globe, Atlas Grotesk typeface, green dots on a dark globe, Dutch Design Award winner; the interface is "everything could be left out, raw and large as life."
- **LoFi Girl** — 24/7 animated YouTube livestream, a real café opening in Paris in 2026, Lego and Chess.com partnerships. The clearest proof that a single looping aesthetic can become infrastructure.

**Lia's world intersects here** every day. She plays SomaFM in the shared kitchen. She has Radio Garden bookmarked. LoFi Girl plays on a second monitor in half the coworking spaces she's been in. The radio metaphor is *native* to her listening habits — the studio metaphor is not.

**Trajectory.** Growing, slowly, in the post-Spotify-fatigue window. LoFi Girl's café deal and ambient-TikTok livestream culture point to radio-as-place, not radio-as-playlist, as the live signal.

## 3. AI-native composition (the camp pulse.city both joins and rejects)

**Definition.** Text-to-audio generative music products where the user describes a vibe and the model returns a finished track. Closed models, opaque outputs.

**Key players / signals (2026).**
- **Suno** — v5.5 (March 2026) adds voice cloning, custom fine-tunes, and a full DAW-style "Studio." Designed by Metalab. Explicitly framed as a web-based "generative audio workstation."
- **Udio** — revamped interface in 2026, three-tab workflow (Generate / Custom / Instrumental), vocals-first positioning.
- The category is loud, funded, and culturally contested (ongoing lawsuits, training-data disclosure fights).

**Lia's world intersects here** adversarially. This is the camp she points at when she says "slot machine." She has tried Suno once, screenshotted a funny lyric, and never opened it again. The visibility of Suno is *useful* — it gives pulse.city a named opposite.

**Trajectory.** Peak hype, cresting into backlash. The 2026 design moves in this category are polish and DAW-ification (Suno is adding stems; Udio is adding custom lyric control) — which means the *opposite* design move (visible code, forkable patterns) is now an actual differentiator rather than a niche preference.

## 4. Pop-up city / co-living soundtrack

**Definition.** Shared audio environments for temporary intentional communities — coworking spaces, hacker houses, pop-up villages, festivals that rent a whole hotel.

**Key players / signals (2026).**
- **Ipê Village 2026** — the real venue, April 22 → May 22, Jurerê Internacional. pulse.city ships for this room.
- **Edge Esmeralda / Edge City** — 1,300 people through a month-long popup in 2024; Zugrama's "Vibes" POAP series explicitly captured "music, co-working, and shared experiences" as the venue's atmosphere layer. An "Open Source Orchestra" POAP (with past editions at Devcon) names permissionless music space as a primitive.
- **Cabin, Zuzalu forks** — ongoing experiments in durational co-living where shared soundscape is a declared-but-unsolved problem. No standard tool exists for "the kitchen is playing."

**Lia's world intersects here** *as a resident.* She is the person the category is building for, and she is also the person who will set up whatever tool runs on the projector. The category has no incumbent.

**Trajectory.** Growing fast from a small base. The pop-up city movement went from one Zuzalu (2023) to a dozen forks in 2024–2025 and an emerging ecosystem (Edge City's PopOut Club) in 2026. "Shared soundtrack" remains an unclaimed infrastructure slot inside this movement.

---

## Where pulse.city sits

At the four-way junction of **live-code legitimacy** (Strudel under the hood), **radio warmth** (SomaFM-style listener posture), **visible-craft AI** (the philosophical opposite of Suno), and **pop-up city infrastructure** (a real venue, a real month, real listeners who will bounce off anything that feels like a SaaS demo). No single competitor stands in all four corners. Strudel owns #1 but has no radio surface and no venue. SomaFM owns #2 but does not generate. Suno owns #3 and is the anti-reference. The pop-up city scene (#4) has no sound tool at all. pulse.city's job is to be legible in all four dialects at once — which is hard, and which is also the whitespace.
