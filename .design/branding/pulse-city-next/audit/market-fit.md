# Market Fit
> Phase: audit | Brand: pulse-city-next | Generated: 2026-04-15

---

## Position vs. named references

### IPÊ.FM (sibling, closest real comparable)
Same venue, same team, opposite posture: IPÊ.FM is a PWA radio with an AI DJ; pulse.city is a full studio + autopilot + agent surface. The differentiation claim is **"the studio is the radio, paused."** A first-time visitor to pulse.city today sees the studio — there is no /radio surface yet, so the claim is invisible. **Legibility from visuals alone: low.**

### Strudel / TidalCycles / Sonic Pi (live-code lineage)
pulse.city *is* Strudel underneath — it inherits the lineage rather than competing with it. Differentiation claim: **"Strudel with an AI copilot, shareable URLs, and a listener surface."** The terminal-dark aesthetic and JetBrains Mono signal "I am in the family" clearly — this lineage reads immediately, possibly too strongly (risk: reads as "Strudel fork" rather than "Strudel-powered product"). **Legibility: high, maybe too high.**

### SomaFM / Radio Garden (curated ambient radios)
Adjacent — pulse.city's /radio surface will compete here. Differentiation claim: **"the stream is generated live and the code is visible while it plays."** SomaFM's warmth comes from curation, handwritten station descriptions, album art. pulse.city has none of that softness in its current look — it's all terminal, no campfire. **Legibility: low — today a SomaFM listener arriving at pulse.city would bounce.**

### Suno / Udio (AI music generation — opposite philosophy)
Philosophical opponent. Differentiation claim: **"code you can read, fork, and walk away from — not a slot machine."** The visible CodeMirror editor is the single strongest anti-Suno signal pulse.city has, and it's front and center. **Legibility: high.** This is the clearest win in the current visual system.

### Endel / Brain.fm (ambient-for-place generative)
Adjacent reference — Endel's "soundscapes for focus/sleep/place" framing is the closest to pulse.city's "living soundtrack for a venue." Differentiation claim: **"open, collective, visible — not a subscription wellness app."** Endel wins on polish and trust-of-brand; pulse.city's terminal look is a deliberate counter-aesthetic to Endel's soft gradients. **Legibility: medium** — readers who recognize the anti-aesthetic get it; everyone else sees "a dev tool."

## Differentiation gaps

- **"Living soundtrack for a place" is the strongest claim in the brief and is invisible in the product.** Nothing on screen says "venue," "radio," "24/7," or "Ipê Village" except the metadata description that nobody reads. The studio-only surface reads as a dev tool, not a place-aware instrument.

- **"Code stays visible" is legible but undersold.** It's the strongest anti-Suno differentiator and currently framed as infrastructure, not as a value. Lia would recognize it; her friend on a projector would not.

- **"Multi-persona, four surfaces" is completely absent from visual identity.** The brief describes Creator / Listener / Agent / Host as first-class audiences. Today all four see the same studio, which means three of the four are receiving the wrong voice.

- **"MCP-native" — arguably the deepest moat — has zero visual presence.** No icon, no badge, no "agents welcome" surface. The brief calls this out as defensibility and it's invisible.

## Trend alignment (honest 2026 read)

- **Terminal-dark as a style**: on-pace, trending up. 2026 has Are.na, Obsidian, ghosty.app, Radiant Foundation, and every crypto-adjacent brand on this aesthetic. pulse.city is not ahead here — it's in a crowded room. The lime/sky/violet trio on `#0a0e17` is genuinely distinctive *within* that room, but the room itself is not a competitive advantage anymore.

- **Chakra Petch**: slightly behind. It peaked around 2022–2023 as the "cyberpunk-y free font." In 2026 the move is either something more neutral-geometric (Space Grotesk, IBM Plex Sans) or something genuinely editorial (PP Neue Montreal, a grotesk with warmth). Chakra Petch anchors pulse.city to a moment that's receding.

- **JetBrains Mono**: on-pace, safe. Still the default signal for "this is real code."

- **Lowercase manifesto voice** ("the city is playing"): ahead of the curve. The shift from shouty startup voice to lowercase quiet-confidence is still arriving, and pulse.city is already there — but only in the metadata, not in the UI.

- **"Open, autonomous, collective" triad**: on-pace with the post-SaaS / post-AI-hype mood. This language would read as genuine to Lia's cohort; to a corporate buyer it would read as uncommercial (which is fine — Lia is the target).

**Summary:** the visual is not behind, but it's not distinctive-enough-to-be-memorable either. The strongest market moves are in the *voice* and *surface architecture*, not the palette.
